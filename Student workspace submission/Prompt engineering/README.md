# The Samal Eco-Tourism Communication Prompt System #

## 1. System Prompt Template (V3 - Final Optimized) ##

> "Act as a Senior Local Government Communications Strategist specializing in sustainable tourism development in the Davao Region. Your objective is to create a 350-word communication action plan for the Tourism Office of Island Garden City of Samal.

Context: Visitor arrivals have increased during peak travel seasons, creating concerns regarding environmental carrying capacity, coastal ecosystem protection, and community participation in tourism activities.

Constraints: Use a professional government communication tone and Philippine English. Focus exclusively on Samal Island's tourism ecosystem, barangay-level stakeholders, local tourism operators, environmental groups, and community-based enterprises. Do NOT reference international tourism destinations, global tourism rankings, or foreign policy frameworks. Avoid marketing buzzwords and corporate jargon.

Format: Output in Markdown. Include the sections:

Situation Analysis

Objectives

Key Messages

Stakeholder Coordination

Priority Interventions (exactly three actionable interventions)

Monitoring Indicators


Every recommendation must explain how it benefits local communities, protects natural resources, and supports sustainable tourism growth within Samal."

## 2. Prompt Battle Ledger ##

Version	Prompt Modifier Added	Output Quality Reflection

V1	"Create a tourism communication plan for Samal."	Too generic. Produced broad tourism recommendations that could apply anywhere in the Philippines.
V2	Added sustainable tourism focus and local government audience.	More relevant, but still included generic environmental recommendations without identifying local stakeholders or implementation mechanisms.
V3	Added geographic lock, barangay-level stakeholders, Philippine English requirement, and exactly three interventions.	Highly localized. Produced government-ready communication outputs aligned with Samal's eco-tourism realities and local governance structure.

## 4. Reusability Notes ##

This prompt system can be adapted to other Davao Region development priorities by replacing the geographic and sector-specific context:

Locality	Sector Focus

Davao del Norte	Agricultural logistics and farm-to-market transport
Davao de Oro	Responsible mining and environmental management
Davao Oriental	Coastal resilience and fisheries development
Davao Occidental	Marine resource conservation
Davao City	Smart governance and public service digitalization
Samal Island	Sustainable eco-tourism and community participation


Design Principle: The geographic lock, stakeholder lock, and tone lock prevent the AI from generating generic, Western-centric outputs and ensure responses remain actionable, localized, and suitable for LGU implementation.
