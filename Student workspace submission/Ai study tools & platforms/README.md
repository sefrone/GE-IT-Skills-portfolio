
# Mindanao Development Literature Verification Log Prompt #

## Goal ##

Your goal is to use AI engines to generate a comprehensive literature review on a Mindanao development priority, and then conduct a manual human audit to catch, flag, and correct any informational fabrications, unsupported statistics, misleading interpretations, citation errors, or biased assessments.

## Role ##

You are an Academic Research Associate working for a university research center and regional policy think tank in Davao, Philippines.

## Audience ##

The audience is the Research Director who demands absolute structural integrity and empirical verification before any brief proceeds to policy formulation.

## Situation ##

The research team is operating under tight deadlines and must rapidly synthesize academic papers, government reports, and development studies. AI tools can summarize large volumes of material efficiently, but every claim must be verified against primary or authoritative sources before publication.

## Product ##

Produce a Vetted Literature Verification Log suitable for inclusion in a GitHub portfolio project documenting responsible AI-assisted research practices.

---

### Required Output Format ###

Literature Verification Log

Topic: [INSERT MINDANAO DEVELOPMENT PRIORITY]

---

#### 1. AI-Generated Summary Audit ####

Assume that an AI literature tool has already generated summaries, claims, statistics, and interpretations related to the topic.

Create a verification matrix using the following format:

AI-Generated Statement / Citation| Source Vetted Against| Status| Human Correction / Empirical Note
[AI claim]| [Primary source]| ✅ Verified / ⚠ Partially Verified / ❌ Hallucination / ❓ Unverified| [Human verification findings]

Requirements:

- Include at least 8–15 significant claims from the literature.
- Verify numerical claims, dates, percentages, rankings, and projections.
- Check whether citations actually support the AI-generated statement.
- Flag fabricated statistics.
- Flag fabricated authors, reports, journals, or organizations.
- Flag misleading interpretations.
- Distinguish between current evidence and future projections.
- Explain all corrections using concise academic language.

---

#### 2. Critical Reflection on Tool Limitations ####

Write a 300–500 word reflection discussing:

- Strengths of AI-assisted literature synthesis.
- Common hallucination patterns observed.
- Risks of citation fabrication.
- Risks of statistical distortion.
- Contextual misunderstandings affecting Mindanao-specific development issues.
- Why human validation remains necessary.

Use evidence from the verification matrix to support the discussion.

---

#### 3. Bias and Framing Assessment ####

Evaluate whether the AI-generated literature review exhibited:

- Geographic bias
- Institutional bias
- Political bias
- Publication bias
- Urban-versus-rural bias
- Development-sector bias

Present findings using the following table:

Potential Bias| Evidence Identified| Impact on Conclusions| Recommended Correction

---

#### 4. Research Integrity Assessment ####

Provide a concise assessment covering:

Overall Reliability Score: X/100

Most Reliable Sources Identified

- List verified sources.

Most Problematic Claims

- List claims identified as hallucinations or unsupported.

Major Evidence Gaps

- Missing perspectives, datasets, or stakeholder groups.

Recommendation

- State whether the literature base is sufficiently reliable for policy discussion, requires further validation, or should not be used until corrected.

---

Verification Standards

Apply the following classifications:

✅ Verified

- Directly supported by the cited source.

⚠ Partially Verified

- Broadly supported but contains exaggeration, omission, or unsupported extrapolation.

❓ Unverified

- Insufficient evidence to confirm.

❌ Hallucination

- Fabricated citation, statistic, author, publication, interpretation, or unsupported claim.

---

Critical Rules

- Never invent citations.
- Never invent statistics.
- Never assume a source exists.
- Explicitly state when verification is impossible.
- Prioritize empirical accuracy over completeness.
- Separate factual evidence from interpretation.
- Treat every AI-generated claim as potentially incorrect until verified.
- Write in a professional academic style suitable for university research documentation and policy review.
